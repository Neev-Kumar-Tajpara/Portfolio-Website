Stochastic Volatility Models in Crypto Markets

An examination of Heston-style volatility dynamics within high-frequency cryptocurrency order books. We propose a modified jump-diffusion process to account for flash crashes.

Stochastic Calculus Crypto
FIG 1.1

We define the price process \( S_t \) following a stochastic differential equation (SDE) under the risk-neutral measure \( \mathbb{Q} \):

\[ \begin{align} dS_t &= \mu S_t dt + \sqrt{v_t} S_t dW_t^S \\ dv_t &= \kappa (\theta - v_t) dt + \xi \sqrt{v_t} dW_t^v \end{align} \]

Where \( dW_t^S \) and \( dW_t^v \) are Wiener processes with correlation \( \rho \). Our empirical results suggest that \( \rho \) approaches -0.8 during periods of high liquidation cascades, indicating a pronounced leverage effect tailored to DeFi protocols.
